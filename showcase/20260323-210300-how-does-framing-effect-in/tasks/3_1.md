To understand the friction between Western journalistic practices and holistic cognitive architectures, we must analyze the specific **markers** that trigger the "Mismatch Response" in a reader. 

This analysis categorizes these markers into two domains: the **Episodic Frame** (the Western input) and the **Contextual Expectation** (the holistic requirement).

---

### I. Cognitive Markers: Western-Centric Episodic Framing
Western media relies on a specific "syntactic" structure designed to facilitate rapid Analytic ingestion. These markers serve to isolate events in space and time.

*   **The "Agent-Action-Outcome" Triad:** The headline and the lead paragraph are constructed as a closed causal loop. (e.g., "Governor X signs bill to lower taxes").
    *   *Cognitive Marker:* **Linear Determinism.** The frame implies that the "Agent" is the sole possessor of agency.
*   **The "Snapshot" Temporal Anchor:** Stories are anchored to a discrete moment (e.g., "As of today," "Following the announcement..."). 
    *   *Cognitive Marker:* **Temporal Atomization.** The frame excludes the "before" and "after," treating the event as an isolated point in time rather than a phase in a process.
*   **The "Zero-Sum" Binary:** Conflicts are presented as binary sets (e.g., "Pro-Choice vs. Pro-Life," "Market Rally vs. Recession Risk").
    *   *Cognitive Marker:* **Categorical Exclusion.** The frame requires the reader to commit to one of two mutually exclusive logical nodes.
*   **The "Proximal Trigger" Bias:** The framing focuses on the immediate, visible cause rather than systemic or distal factors.
    *   *Cognitive Marker:* **Dispositional Attribution.** The focus is on the "why" of the person/object, rather than the "why" of the environment.

---

### II. Linguistic Markers: Holistic-Contextual Reader Expectations
The holistic reader expects language that signals the "embeddedness" of an event. When these markers are absent, the reader perceives the news as "thin" or "deceptive."

*   **Relational Verbs and Transitions:** Holistic readers look for markers of interdependence (e.g., "Reflecting the broader trend of...", "In alignment with the shifting climate of...", "Echoing previous systemic cycles...").
    *   *Linguistic Expectation:* **Thematic Continuity.** Language that connects the present event to a historical or environmental baseline.
*   **Qualifiers of Complexity:** Holistic readers expect "hedging" or nuanced language that acknowledges the "field."
    *   *Linguistic Expectation:* **The "Web" Marker.** Use of modifiers that suggest systemic pressure rather than singular agency (e.g., "Amidst rising pressures," "Within the context of a maturing industry," "As part of a multifaceted policy shift").
*   **Cyclical Temporal Indicators:** Language that places events on a continuum rather than a snapshot.
    *   *Linguistic Expectation:* **Duration-Based Lexicon.** Use of words like "recurrent," "fluctuating," "evolved," and "integrated" to signal that the event is a point on a curve, not an isolated explosion.
*   **The "Field-Inclusion" Clause:** Phrases that denote the surrounding environment as an active participant.
    *   *Linguistic Expectation:* **Systemic Agency.** (e.g., "The legislation was shaped by the mounting sentiment in the manufacturing sector," rather than "Governor X signed the bill.")

### IV. Synthesis: The "Translation Tax" on Language
When an Analytic/Episodic news report reaches a holistic reader, the "translation tax" is essentially the **mental labor required to re-insert the missing linguistic markers.** 

*   **If the report says:** *"Company A fired 500 employees to boost quarterly profit."*
*   **The Analytic Processor reads:** "The Agent (Company) took an Action (Firing) to achieve an Outcome (Profit)." (Transaction complete).
*   **The Holistic Reader reads:** "The Agent is an isolated actor." (Dissonance). The reader then searches for the *missing linguistic markers*: "What is the industry trajectory?" "What is the unemployment cycle?" "What is the social impact on the community?" 

**Strategic Recommendation:** To bridge this marker gap, information producers must adopt **"Contextual Framing"**—specifically by including "Systemic Markers" within the first three paragraphs of any report. This involves using language that explicitly shifts the subject from the *Agent* to the *Environment* (e.g., "Against a backdrop of industry-wide consolidation, Company A's decision to downsize reflects a broader, cyclical pressure to optimize..."). By providing these markers, the sender lowers the Translation Tax and reduces the Holistic reader’s urge to reject the framing as "reductive."
