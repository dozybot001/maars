# Information Processing Model: News Header Ingestion

This model maps the cognitive path from stimulus (the news header) to cognitive resolution (the creation of meaning). It assumes that the "news header" is an ambiguous, compressed packet of information that triggers diverging processing pathways based on the viewer’s cognitive and communicative orientation.

---

## 1. The Stimulus: The Compressed Header
A news header is an **"Information Atom."** It is designed to maximize reach by minimizing detail.
*   **The Analytic/Explicit expectation:** The header *is* the object. It should contain the Who, What, Where, and When.
*   **The Holistic/Implicit expectation:** The header is a *portal* to a broader context. It suggests the mood, the trend, and the relational gravity of the story.

---

## 2. The Processing Sequence (Decision Nodes)

The following sequence outlines the cognitive flow after the eyes register the headline.

### Stage 1: Initial Sensory Gating
*   **Node A (Filtering):** Does the viewer scan for *keywords* (nouns/actors) or *patterns* (themes/narratives)?
    *   **Analytic path:** Gating focuses on **Object Recognition**. The mind parses the headline as a set of logical variables.
    *   **Holistic path:** Gating focuses on **Contextual Appraisal**. The mind parses the headline as an indicator of a changing social or global environment.

### Stage 2: The Cognitive Branching Point (The "Meaning" Node)
Once the header is read, the brain must resolve the "information gap" inherent in headlines.

*   **Analytic Branch (Dispositional/Categorical):**
    *   *Decision:* "Is this a fact or an opinion?"
    *   *Operation:* The mind attempts to separate the object (the actor) from the claim.
    *   *Resolution:* The header is filed into a discrete logical category (e.g., "Politics: Legislation Passed").
    *   *Outcome:* **Information Verification.** The user seeks secondary data to "confirm" the object.

*   **Holistic Branch (Situational/Relational):**
    *   *Decision:* "How does this change the 'field' of my current knowledge?"
    *   *Operation:* The mind attempts to connect the headline to existing, unrelated events, history, or social sentiment.
    *   *Resolution:* The header is filed into an integrated thematic map (e.g., "Crisis: A sign of systemic instability").
    *   *Outcome:* **Information Synthesis.** The user seeks secondary data to "flesh out" the atmospheric nuance.

### Stage 3: The Attribution Node
How the brain assigns "Why did this happen?"

*   **Analytic Node:** Focuses on **Causal Agency**. The header is read to identify a "who" (a prime mover). *If the header lacks an actor, the Analytic processor experiences cognitive dissonance.*
*   **Holistic Node:** Focuses on **Systems Dynamics**. The header is read to identify the "flow." *If the header focuses too heavily on a single actor, the Holistic processor finds it reductive and likely biased.*

---

## 3. Comparative Path Matrix: The "Headline Ingestion" Model

| Decision Node | Analytic/Explicit Pathway | Holistic/Implicit Pathway |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Input Focus** | Keyword extraction (Actors/Nouns) | Emotional/Thematic tone (Field) |
| **Cognitive Goal** | Accuracy of discrete facts | Integrity of the broader narrative |
| **Primary Question** | "What actually happened?" | "What is the meaning of this change?" |
| **Resolution Type** | Logical verification (Truth/False) | Consonance/Dissonance (Harmonious) |
| **Failure Mode** | Perceives "nuance" as obfuscation | Perceives "fact-stripping" as deception |

---

## 4. Synthesis: Implications for Information Design

This model reveals why "clickbait" or "vague-scrolling" works differently across these cohorts:

1.  **For the Analytic Processor:** The "Headline" is an interface. When it fails to provide clear object-data (e.g., "The Truth About [X]"), they feel a high degree of cognitive stress, leading them to either click to "find the object" or dismiss the source entirely.
2.  **For the Holistic Processor:** The "Headline" is an orientation tool. When it is too clinical or mechanical, they experience a lack of resonance, leading them to search for "contextual" indicators (e.g., reading the comment section to gauge the *field's* reaction before engaging with the article itself).

### Strategic Recommendation for Cross-Cognitive Communication:
To reach both groups, news synthesis must provide **"The Explicit Hook"** (the analytic requirement: a clear, fact-based summary) followed immediately by **"The Field Context"** (the holistic requirement: an explanation of why this matter matters in the current environment). Neglecting either creates a "Meaning Gap" that the reader will fill with their own biased, culturally-conditioned assumptions.
