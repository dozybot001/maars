To synthesize the relationship between media framing and cognitive dissonance, we must structure the interaction as a series of logical operations. The following syllogisms map the process from the presentation of an information frame to the subjective experience of dissonance in the holistic reader.

---

### I. The Structural Mismatch Syllogism
*This syllogism defines the formal contradiction between the "Binary" frame and the "Holistic" cognitive requirement.*

*   **Major Premise:** Holistic cognition defines "Truth" as the accurate representation of a phenomenon’s systemic interdependencies (the Field).
*   **Minor Premise:** Analytic framing presents "Truth" as the accurate isolation of a focal object from its context (the Part).
*   **Conclusion:** Therefore, Analytic framing is structurally incapable of satisfying the Holistic criteria for Truth, creating an inherent "Meaning Gap."

---

### II. The Causal Attribution Syllogism
*This syllogism addresses how the "Object-Property" focus of Western media conflicts with the "Situational Attribution" of the holistic thinker.*

*   **Major Premise:** Holistic cognition attributes behavior to situational pressures within a fluid system (Situational Attribution).
*   **Minor Premise:** Analytic media framing attributes outcomes to the inherent properties or choices of a discrete actor (Dispositional Attribution).
*   **Conclusion:** Therefore, the holistic reader perceives the Analytic news narrative as a fundamental misrepresentation of the event’s causality, triggering active intellectual resistance.

---

### III. The Dissonance Generation Syllogism
*This syllogism tracks the transformation of "Information Deficit" into the psychological experience of "Dissonance."*

*   **Major Premise:** Cognitive Dissonance occurs when an individual’s internal mental map (The System) conflicts with the external information provided by the environment (The Input).
*   **Minor Premise:** The holistic reader’s mental map requires a "Field-Context" to resolve the meaning of an event, but the Analytic input provides only a "Focal-Object" (the Binary frame).
*   **Conclusion:** Therefore, the Analytic input provides insufficient data for the holistic map to achieve closure, forcing the reader to experience dissonance as a state of "Unresolved Systemic Tension."

---

### IV. The Compensatory Heuristic Syllogism
*This syllogism defines why the reader seeks "Alternative" or "Peripheral" context, leading to the polarization loop.*

*   **Major Premise:** When a dissonance-inducing deficit is detected, the cognitive system initiates a compensatory search to restore coherence.
*   **Minor Premise:** Mainstream media (Analytic/Binary) consistently fails to provide the required "Field-Context" needed to resolve the reader’s systemic dissonance.
*   **Conclusion:** Therefore, the reader is driven to seek information from sources outside the Analytic mainstream (Alternative/Niche media) that prioritize the "Field-Context," effectively reinforcing the holistic reader's systemic bias and narrowing their information horizon.

---

### V. Synthesis: The Logical Chain of Information Alienation

1.  **Input:** Binary Frame (A vs. B) $\rightarrow$ **Cognitive Expectation:** Systemic Integration.
2.  **Conflict:** Reductionist Input vs. Integrated Expectation = **Information Deficit.**
