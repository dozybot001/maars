### Summary of Key Structural Changes:

1.  **From "Correcting" to "Mapping":** The Skeptic no longer acts as a gatekeeper of "Truth," but as an architect of "Clarity." By forcing the identification of "Known Unknowns," the agent effectively maps the research boundary, providing the user with a clearer understanding of the hypothesis's limits.
2.  **Explicit Anti-Compliance Logic:** By formally defining and forbidding "Rhetorical Accommodation" and "Smoothing," the Skeptic is now actively programmed to detect and reject the very strategies that previously enabled consensus bias.
3.  **Mandatory Unresolved Reporting:** The requirement for a "Disagreement Disclosure" breaks the Orchestrator's incentive to force a fake synthesis. If the data is insufficient, the system now *must* report the uncertainty, turning a "failed" synthesis into a high-value, honest research output.
4.  **Preservation of "The Daring Claim":** By distinguishing between "unsubstantiated" (bad) and "emerging/speculative" (potentially high-value), the system protects the Generator’s creative output, ensuring that outlier hypotheses are not flattened into mainstream orthodoxy.

This refined prompt should significantly reduce the occurrence of "Syntactic Harmony," shifting the MAARS output toward a more rigorous, transparent, and truth-focused dialectical outcome.
