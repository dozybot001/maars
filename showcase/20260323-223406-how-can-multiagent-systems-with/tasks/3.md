# Documentation: Generation Phase Workflow (MAARS Architecture)

## 1. Overview
The Generation Phase is the primary creative and analytical engine of the MAARS architecture. Its objective is to transform raw domain-specific corpora into synthesized, structured intelligence. Operating under the "Independence Principle," this phase ensures that Workers produce unbiased, high-fidelity outputs before these outputs are subjected to the adversarial auditing of the subsequent Dialectical Loop stages.

## 2. Worker Access Protocols (Corpora Interaction)

To maintain systemic integrity, Workers do not interact with raw datasets arbitrarily. Access is mediated through a structured **Ingestion and Context-Locking Layer**:

1.  **Scope Definition**: Upon task initiation, the Orchestrator assigns a specific sub-domain boundary to the Worker. The Worker is granted read-only access to a virtualized slice of the corpora relevant to that scope.
2.  **Context Loading (The Sandbox)**: The corpus is pre-processed into a vector space, ensuring the Worker operates within a "sandboxed context." This prevents cross-domain contamination and ensures focus on domain-specific terminology and precedent.
3.  **Traceability Metadata**: Every generation task must attach metadata to its output, detailing which nodes of the corpora were consulted. This ensures that the later Auditing Phase can perform recursive fact-checking against source material.

## 3. The Synthesis Workflow

The Generation Phase follows a four-step internal progression:

### Phase 3.1: Decomposition and Mapping
Workers first break down the research query into atomic analytical components. They perform an initial mapping of the retrieved corpora segments against these components, creating a "Knowledge Heatmap."
*   *Action*: Identification of high-relevance versus low-relevance source documents.
*   *Requirement*: Flagging of gaps where domain-specific corpora fail to support a component of the query.

### Phase 3.2: Dialectical Draft Generation (Internal)
Before finalizing, the Worker simulates an internal heuristic check. 
*   *Constraint*: The Worker must formulate at least two competing hypotheses for every significant finding. This forces the agent to acknowledge potential contradictions inherent in the corpus before submitting its "best estimate."

### Phase 3.3: Canonical Structuring
The synthesized information must be structured according to the **MAARS Standard Output Schema**:
*   **Executive Synthesis**: The distilled conclusion.
*   **Evidential Chain**: A structured list of supporting data points tied to specific corpus entries.
*   **Confidence Scoring**: A quantitative assessment (0.0–1.0) based on source convergence and data density.
*   **Anomaly Report**: A section dedicated to outliers or conflicting information found within the corpora.

### Phase 3.4: Integrity Encapsulation
The final output is encapsulated in a cryptographically signed document format that prevents modification until it reaches the Auditing Phase.

## 4. Operational Guardrails

To prevent hallucination and ensure logical consistency:
*   **Hard Constraints**: If a query cannot be answered with the assigned corpora, the Worker is prohibited from extrapolating. It must return a "Null-Result: Insufficient Domain Data" flag.
*   **Reference Anchoring**: Every claim made in the generation must be mapped back to a specific index within the provided corpora. Unanchored claims are discarded during the pre-audit validation check.
*   **Temporal Stamping**: All synthesis must account for the temporal validity of the corpora. Workers are instructed to prioritize recent data while acknowledging historical context where applicable.

## 5. Transition to Auditing
Once the Generation Phase completes the Integrity Encapsulation, the output is passed to the **Integration Buffer**. At this point, the Worker is disconnected from the corpora access stream to ensure the integrity of the subsequent Audit Phase, which must operate on the *output* of the generation, not the source data itself.
