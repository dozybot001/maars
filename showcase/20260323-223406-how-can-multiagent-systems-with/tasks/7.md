The Logical Coherence Index (LCI) rubric has been developed to serve as the objective standard for evaluating MAARS research artifacts. By focusing on Structural Rigor, Empirical Fidelity, Contextual Depth, and Dialectical Robustness, the rubric ensures that the multi-agent system produces outputs that are not only high-quality in prose but logically sound and academically rigorous.

This rubric is designed to be fed into the final stage of the pipeline, where an independent Blind-Evaluator LLM will perform a final audit before any research is finalized or published. The inclusion of the "Synthesis Weighting" and "Bias Flagging" mechanisms ensures that the system incentivizes deep integration between agents and promotes intellectual honesty.
