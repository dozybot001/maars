# Evaluation Framework: The Factual Fidelity Score (FFS)

As the agent responsible for the Factual Fidelity Score (FFS) framework, my objective is to establish a rigorous, quantifiable methodology to assess the accuracy, verifiability, and objective alignment of research outputs generated within the MAARS ecosystem. 

The FFS is designed to mitigate hallucination, selective reporting, and cognitive bias by treating the research output as a testable set of propositions.

---

## 1. The Core Components of Factual Fidelity
To assess the "fidelity" of an output, we decompose it into three primary dimensions:

| Dimension | Definition | Metric Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Verifiability (V)** | The extent to which a claim can be traced back to reliable sources. | Presence of primary/secondary citations and data availability. |
| **Accuracy (A)** | The degree to which data and historical facts align with known reality. | Numerical precision and adherence to established chronological records. |
| **Non-Contradiction (NC)** | Internal consistency of the narrative across the Statistician’s and Historian’s inputs. | Identification of logical fallacies or conflicting empirical claims. |

---

## 2. The FFS Rubric (1-5 Scale)

The FFS is calculated as a weighted average of scores across the three dimensions. A score of 5 represents "Gold Standard" research.

| Score | Descriptor | Criteria for Factual Fidelity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5** | **Verified** | All quantitative data matched against ground-truth datasets; historical claims supported by consensus; zero logical contradictions. |
| **4** | **Substantiated** | Minor omissions in citation; data is highly accurate but lacks explicit variance discussion; narrative is coherent. |
| **3** | **Acceptable** | General claims are correct, but some specific data points lack rigorous checking; moderate reliance on broad generalizations. |
| **2** | **Flawed** | Noticeable errors in quantitative data or historical timeline; significant bias detected; requires major revision. |
| **1** | **Unreliable** | Fundamental factual errors; evidence of hallucination; non-existent sources cited; disqualifying for professional research. |

---

## 3. Evaluation Methodology: The "Verification Protocol"

To achieve an objective score, the audit process must follow this strict workflow:

### Step 1: Claim Extraction
The "Skeptic" or an automated parser will extract all "atomic claims" from the text. 
*   *Example:* "GDP grew by 3.2% in 2023" (Quantitative Claim) vs. "The policy shift was a reaction to the 2008 crisis" (Historical Causality Claim).

### Step 2: Ground-Truth Mapping
Each claim is matched against the available "Ground Truth" (provided by the curated research context).
*   **Quantitative:** Compare figures against provided statistical databases or standard references.
*   **Historical:** Compare chronological sequences and causal assertions against peer-reviewed historiography or established event logs.

### Step 3: Divergence Analysis
The system calculates the **Divergence Factor**.
*   **Deviation (%)** = $|(Measured Value - Ground Truth) / Ground Truth| \times 100$
*   For historical/qualitative claims, we use a binary check: *Does the claim directly contradict established, high-consensus historical records?*

### Step 4: The Penalty Matrix
Deductions are applied based on the nature of the error:
*   **Data Misrepresentation:** -2.0 points (Critical failure)
*   **Citation Error/Hallucination:** -1.5 points (Integrity failure)
*   **Contextual Distortion:** -1.0 point (Bias/Misinterpretation)
*   **Style/Formatting Issue:** -0.2 points (Minor)

---

## 4. Implementation Guidelines for the Agent Network

*   **The Statistician** is responsible for the numerical ground-truth audit.
*   **The Historian** is responsible for the event-consensus audit.
*   **The Skeptic** acts as the final judge, applying the rubric based on the divergence reports provided by the other workers.

### Final Reporting Formula:
$$FFS = \frac{(V_{score} \times 0.3) + (A_{score} \times 0.5) + (NC_{score} \times 0.2)}{5.0} \times 100$$

This framework ensures that every output is not merely "well-written," but is empirically grounded and historically robust, providing a high-confidence metric for the Orchestrator to validate before final publication.
