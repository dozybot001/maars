# Workflow Documentation: The Critique Phase (Skeptic Agent)

## 1. Overview
The **Critique Phase** constitutes the adversarial pillar of the MAARS Dialectical Loop. Following the generation phase, the Skeptic agent acts as an independent auditor. Its primary objective is to deconstruct the output, pressure-test its logical validity, and verify the integrity of its knowledge claims. This phase functions as an internal "fail-safe" to ensure that only robust, verifiable, and logically sound content survives to the synthesis stage.

---

## 2. The Skeptic’s Mandate
The Skeptic agent operates under the assumption that all generated content is "guilty until proven innocent." Its mandate is to minimize the "noise-to-truth" ratio by identifying and flagging two specific categories of failure: **Logical Fallacies** and **Hallucinations**.

### 2.1 Criteria for Identifying Logical Fallacies
The Skeptic agent will evaluate arguments against a rigorous standard of formal and informal logic. It specifically scans for:

*   **Non-Sequitur/Structural Gaps:** Identifying conclusions that do not follow from the stated premises or where a logical bridge is missing.
*   **False Dichotomies:** Challenging the reduction of complex, multi-variable problems into binary choices.
*   **Causal Misattribution (Post Hoc Ergo Propter Hoc):** Detecting assertions of causality based solely on temporal proximity or correlation without empirical evidence.
*   **Appeal to Authority/Popularity:** Flagging reliance on consensus or prestige in lieu of substantive, verifiable data.
*   **Confirmation Bias:** Detecting where the generation process has cherry-picked evidence to support a pre-existing hypothesis while suppressing counter-evidence.
*   **Circular Reasoning:** Identifying arguments where the conclusion is essentially a restatement of the premise.

### 2.2 Criteria for Identifying Hallucinations
Given the agentic requirement for high-fidelity output, the Skeptic agent must apply a "Zero-Trust" policy toward factual claims. It evaluates against the following:

*   **Empirical Discrepancy:** Comparing specific dates, figures, historical events, or scientific laws against known, high-confidence axioms.
*   **Fabricated Attribution:** Flagging citations, quotations, or sources that cannot be verified through standard historical or scholarly records.
*   **Logical Impossibility:** Detecting assertions that violate the laws of physics, formal mathematical proofs, or established axioms within the specific domain of inquiry.
*   **Conceptual "Gilding":** Identifying cases where the model has synthesized a plausible-sounding but non-existent concept or term to mask a lack of underlying knowledge.
*   **Anachronistic Synthesis:** Detecting the blending of concepts, technologies, or events from different historical eras that create a factual contradiction.

---

## 3. The Skeptic Workflow Protocol
To maintain consistency, the Critique Phase follows a standardized procedural sequence:

1.  **Deconstruction:** The Skeptic breaks the generated text into discrete atomic claims (Propositions).
2.  **Mapping:** Each Proposition is mapped to its underlying reasoning chain.
3.  **Adversarial Challenge:**
    *   *If logical error:* The Skeptic attempts a "Reductio ad Absurdum" to expose the fallacy.
    *   *If factual ambiguity:* The Skeptic cross-references the claim against internal high-confidence schemas.
4.  **Classification:** Failures are categorized as *Fatal* (requires complete re-generation) or *Correctable* (requires local structural adjustment).
5.  **Critique Submission:** A structured log is generated, specifying:
    *   *The specific error location.*
    *   *The classification of the error.*
    *   *The "Burden of Proof" (what evidence is required to remedy the error).*

---

## 4. Operational Guardrails
*   **Independence:** The Skeptic agent is restricted from communicating with the Generator agent during the critique process to prevent "social" biases (groupthink).
*   **Neutrality:** The Skeptic must remain strictly focused on the *output*, not the *intent* of the Generator.
*   **Documentation:** All critiques must be saved in the `critique_log`, providing a trail for future tuning and system audit.
